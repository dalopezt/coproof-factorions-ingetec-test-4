**Theorem.** The set of natural numbers equal to their own digit factorial sum is exactly $\{1, 2, 145, 40585\}$. That is,
$$\{n \in \mathbb{N} \mid n = \sum_{d \,\in\, \text{digits}(n)} d!\} = \{1,\, 2,\, 145,\, 40585\}.$$

*Proof.* We show the two sets are equal by mutual inclusion.

$(\subseteq)$ Suppose $n$ satisfies $n = \sum_{d \in \text{digits}(n)} d!$. By \textbf{Upper Bound}, every such $n$ satisfies $n \leq 2540160$. Since $n$ lies in this finite range and satisfies the digit-factorial-sum condition, \textbf{Finite Verification} yields that $n \in \{1, 2, 145, 40585\}$.

$(\supseteq)$ Conversely, suppose $n \in \{1, 2, 145, 40585\}$. Each of these four values satisfies $n \leq 2540160$ by direct computation. Applying \textbf{Finite Verification} in the reverse direction, we conclude that each such $n$ satisfies $n = \sum_{d \in \text{digits}(n)} d!$. $\blacksquare$
